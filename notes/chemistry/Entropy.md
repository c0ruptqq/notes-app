Entropy is a measure of the dispersal of energy and therefore of disorder (the number of ways in which something can be arranged) in a system. A system such as a room is in a state of high entropy when its degree of disorder is high. (Second Law of of Thermodynamics):
- Overall entropy in universe is always increasing

- Double bonds restrict the movement of molecules, so they are "smaller" compared to single bond molecules ei. propene is "smaller" than propane
- To calculate entropy change $\Delta S = \Delta(\text{Products})- \Delta(\text{Reactants})$

### Gibbs Free Energy 

Gibbs Free Energy $\Delta G$ is a combination of whether or not a reaction will occur.
$\Delta G = \Delta H - T \Delta S_{\text{system}}$
- $\Delta H$ - Enthalpy change ($J mol^{-1}$)
- $\Delta S_{\text{system}}$ -Entropy change of the system ($JK^{-1}mol^{-1}$)
- T - Temperature (K)
If $\Delta G\leq 0$ , he reaction will be feasible
