Motor Effect

When a current-charging conductor is places near an external magnet, it will experience a magnetic force. We apply Fleming's left-hand-rule to find the direction of magnetic force

![Pasted image 20240607120326.png](Pasted%20image%2020240607120326.png)

$F (Newtons) =B(Tesla)I(Ampere)L(Meters)\sin \theta$

Theta is the angle between the wire and the the B-Field (Magnetic field)
L is the length exposed to the magnet, not the total length

Definition of magnetic flux density (B)* (Also magnetic field strength)

$B = \frac{F}{IL}$

Defined as force per unit length per unit current on a current-carrying wire at 90* to the magnetic field lines.

When a charged particle moves through a magnetic field it experiences a magnetic force, such that the direction of the force and the velocity of the particle are perpendicular $F \perp v$. If $F \perp v$ then the particle moves along a circular path. 


$$\frac{mv^2}{r}=BQv$$
$$v=\frac{BQr}{m}$$
$$\frac{2\pi r}{T}=\frac{BQr}{m}$$
$$T=\frac{2\pi m}{BQ}$$
